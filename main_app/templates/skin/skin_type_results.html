Normal Skin

Your skin is well-balanced, with neither excessive oil nor dry patches. It feels smooth and has a regular texture with minimal imperfections.

Care Tips:

  • Maintain your skin’s health with a balanced skincare routine that includes gentle cleansing, regular moisturizing, and sun protection.
  • Occasional light exfoliation can help maintain its glow.
{% elif skin_type == 'Dry' %}

Dry Skin

Your skin often feels tight and may exhibit flakiness or scaling. It can appear dull due to a lack of moisture and might be more prone to sensitivity.

Care Tips:

  • Focus on hydration by using rich moisturizers and creams that contain hyaluronic acid and ceramides.
  • Avoid hot showers and harsh cleansers that can strip your skin of its natural oils.
{% elif skin_type == 'Oily' %}

Oily Skin

Your skin tends to produce excess oil, leading to a shiny complexion and making it prone to breakouts and enlarged pores.

Care Tips:

  • Use oil-free and non-comedogenic products to avoid clogging pores.
  • Incorporate a regular cleansing routine, use a gentle exfoliant to manage oil levels, and don’t skip moisturizer—opt for a lightweight, water-based formula.
{% elif skin_type == 'Combination' %}

Combination Skin

Combination skin features a mix of oily and dry zones, typically with oiliness in the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) and dryness on the cheeks.

Care Tips:

  • Treat different areas with targeted products—lighter, oil-control products for the T-zone and richer moisturizers for dry areas.
  • Balancing the skin’s needs can be key to managing combination skin effectively.
{% elif skin_type == 'Sensitive' %}

Sensitive Skin

Your skin is reactive and can be easily irritated by environmental factors, products, or stress, often resulting in redness, itching, or rashes.

Care Tips:

  • Opt for hypoallergenic and fragrance-free products to minimize potential irritations.
  • Be gentle with your skin, avoiding aggressive exfoliants and opting for soothing ingredients like aloe vera and chamomile.
